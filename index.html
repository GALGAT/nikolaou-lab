About the Nikolaou's Research Group

Our research aims at the development of fundamental methods for the intelligent design and operation of engineered systems, translating theoretical frameworks into solutions for real-world problems. Whether dealing with a chemical plant, hydrocarbon production, or a medicated human patient, intelligent design offers distinct advantages in safety, quality, reliability, and performance.

We employ quantitative analysis tools—including mathematical modeling, optimization, statistics, and feedback control—to build the "brains" of these systems. With a strong interdisciplinary flavor, our work relies on collaboration with domain experts and field practitioners to test solutions via computer simulations, laboratory experiments, and full-scale implementations.
